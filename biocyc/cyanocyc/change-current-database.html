WHy and how to change the current database (short)

Click here for longer description

What is a "database"?: It's an organism, its genome, and everything BioCyc knows about it.

Why change the database?: Many BioCyc operations use a single organism as a starting off point. For example, gene searches take place within the organism you choose.

How to change the database?

  1. Gene / protein pages and many other pages have a butten near the top of the page Change Current Database. Click it.
     
  2. This will bring up a Select an Organism Database dialog box. There are several ways to indicate the desired Organism:
     
    • By Name: Specify any part of the name of the desired organism. If the organism has a strain number (e.g. 7120 in Anabaena/Nostoc PCC 7120, typing that in should be enough to evoke the strain you want. Then click the name.
       
    • By Taxonomy: Opening up the Taxonomy tab allows you to go through a taxonomic tree to find the desired organism, using + signs to expand taxonomic groups. Note that cyanobacteria are found within the Terrabacteria group. When you find the desired organism, select it by clicking its name.
       
  3. Press the OK button in the lower right corner of the dialog box.
