There is a subscription fee for extended use of CyanoCyc... why?
EcoCyc (covering E. coli) and MetaCyc (covering all domains of life from afar) were developed with public funding and are freely available to the public. BioCyc (covering thousands of organisms) and CyanoCyc (covering cyanobacteria) were not (though not for want of trying), yet they also need to pay for servers and support people. Somehow.

At present, the cost of supporting CyanoCyc is borne by those who use it. Biocyc is constantly looking for ways of reducing the burden on individual researchers, perhaps at some point eliminating the cost of subscription altogether. The more people actively use BioCyc, the more this becomes possible.
